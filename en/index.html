Wine, Territory, and Culture

Wine is best understood when told from where it comes from: the landscape, the climate, and the people behind it.

I work across Patagonia, integrating communication, experience, and strategy to position wine as a cultural expression and a driver of regional identity.

My approach is built on real presence in the territory, connecting vineyards, producers, gastronomy, and audiences through lived experience rather than theory.

A perspective that starts in the territory and projects to the world.

Projects & Experience

This way of understanding wine takes shape through real projects where territory, gastronomy, and people come together in meaningful experiences.

Vino a la Montaña

Co-creator · Concept Direction

I co-create Vino a la Montaña, a Patagonian wine gathering held in San Martín de los Andes.

Beyond being an event, it is a platform where wine is experienced at its source, connecting producers, landscape, gastronomy, and culture.

It offers a unique opportunity to discover the diversity of Patagonian wines in one place.

The project is currently evolving toward its 2026 edition, with the goal of strengthening Patagonia’s position as a distinctive wine territory in the global conversation.

Patagonia Argentina · National and international projection

About

I am Sergio Landoni, a territory-based sommelier working from Patagonia Argentina.

I bridge the gap between the origins of Patagonian wine and audiences seeking authenticity, connecting the vineyard to the global market.

I work directly within the territory, in vineyards, wineries, and gastronomic projects, with a close understanding of both production and experience.

This perspective allows me to interpret wine beyond technical analysis, positioning it within its cultural and geographical context.

What I bring

  • Identity — territorial storytelling and positioning.
  • Connection — integration between wine, gastronomy, and culture.
  • Projection — connection to international networks and markets.

My work contributes to the international recognition of emerging wine regions like Patagonia, aligning with the global standards of excellence found in competitions such as the International Wine & Spirit Competition (IWSC), where context, typicity, and regional identity are essential to modern wine assessment.

This is the perspective that guides every project I develop.
