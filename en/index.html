Patagonia Argentina · A distinctive origin with global projection

Wine begins in the place

Wine does not begin in the glass. It begins in the place.

In Patagonia, this truth is undeniable. The landscapes, the constant wind, the local cuisine and the way of life shape the character of the wines long before they are poured.

My work focuses on understanding and communicating this connection — not from theory, but directly from the territory. I collaborate with wineries, gastronomy and hospitality projects, translating real experiences into clear and authentic narratives.

With deep knowledge of Neuquén’s six distinctive wine regions and Patagonia’s cool-climate expressions, I communicate the identity of these wines to international audiences from within the region itself.

Based in San Martín de los Andes, Neuquén, I specialise in Patagonian wines, with a particular focus on the Neuquén wine region — one of Argentina’s most distinctive cool-climate areas.

As a territory-based sommelier, I work from origin, helping position Patagonian wines through content, experiences and strategic communication rooted in place.

About Sergio Landoni

I am Sergio Landoni, a territory-based sommelier working from Patagonia, Argentina.

My work focuses on understanding and communicating wine from its place of origin — connecting Neuquén and Patagonian wines with international audiences, professionals and the wine trade.

Rather than approaching wine only from a technical perspective, I work directly within the territory, alongside wineries, producers and gastronomy, interpreting wine through landscape, climate and cultural context.

I specialise in the Neuquén wine region, one of Argentina’s most distinctive cool-climate areas, contributing to its positioning within the global wine conversation.

I work from within Patagonia, building a direct connection between producers, gastronomy and the broader wine conversation, helping communicate these wines with clarity and authenticity.

What I offer

  • Territorial identity — positioning and storytelling rooted in place.
  • Connection — linking wine, gastronomy, tourism and culture.
  • International projection — bridging Patagonia with global wine audiences.

My approach helps communicate Patagonia not only as a wine region, but as a distinctive origin defined by freshness, precision and identity.
