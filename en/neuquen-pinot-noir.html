Neuquén's Pinot Noir: Patagonia's Emerging Expression of Elegance

An insider's look at one of Argentina's most distinctive cool-climate wine expressions.

By Sergio Landoni, Territory-Based Sommelier

Published: June 30, 2026 · Updated: June 30, 2026

When wine lovers think about Argentine Patagonia, many immediately picture vast landscapes, snow-capped mountains, and endless steppes swept by strong winds. Fewer know that, in the heart of this remote region, a distinctive expression of Pinot Noir has been quietly gaining recognition.

While Patagonia has long been associated with cool-climate wines, Neuquén is now establishing itself as one of South America's most exciting frontiers for this elegant variety.

Located in northern Patagonia, the province of Neuquén combines a unique set of natural conditions. The vineyards are planted in an arid environment with low annual rainfall, abundant sunshine, and marked diurnal temperature shifts during the growing season. These conditions allow grapes to ripen slowly while preserving freshness and natural acidity—two essential elements for producing high-quality wines.

The Terroir of San Patricio del Chañar

Most of Neuquén's vineyards are concentrated around San Patricio del Chañar, a region that has become synonymous with premium Patagonian viticulture. Here, the combination of well-drained alluvial soils, cool nights, and constant winds creates wines that are elegant, vibrant, and remarkably expressive.

This regional Pinot Noir typically displays aromas of fresh red cherries, raspberries, wild strawberries, and delicate floral notes. Depending on the producer and the specific site, subtle hints of herbs, spices, and earthy nuances may also emerge. On the palate, these wines combine freshness and tension with fine tannins and a persistent finish, offering a style that international consumers appreciate for its balance and food-friendly character.

Expanding Frontiers: Beyond the Known Map

What makes Neuquén particularly interesting is that the region is still evolving; new vineyards continue to expand our understanding of where the grape can thrive. One of the most intriguing developments is taking place in Plaza Huincul, in the central plateau of Neuquén, where recent plantings have shown promising early results. These pioneering vineyards suggest that the viticultural map of Patagonia may continue to grow in unexpected directions.

Today, wineries such as Familia Schroeder, Malma, Patritti, Familia Aicardi, and several smaller producers are exploring different interpretations of Pinot Noir, demonstrating the diversity that Neuquén can offer within a relatively young wine region.

Pinot Noir and the Patagonia Experience

For travelers, discovering Pinot Noir in Neuquén is also an invitation to experience Patagonia itself. Many wineries welcome visitors and offer tastings surrounded by dramatic landscapes, creating a connection between wine, nature, and local culture that is difficult to replicate elsewhere.

As global consumers increasingly seek authenticity and wines with a strong sense of place, Neuquén's Pinot Noir stands out as a compelling discovery: elegant, distinctive, and deeply rooted in one of the world's most extraordinary wine territories.
