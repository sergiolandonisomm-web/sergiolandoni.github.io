Neuquén Wine Regions, Patagonia’s Emerging Wine Territory

A territorial perspective on landscape, lifestyle, gastronomy and wine in Argentine Patagonia

By Sergio Landoni, Sommelier de Territorio

Neuquén Province, located in Argentine Patagonia, is an emerging wine territory shaped by wind, desert landscapes, Andean rivers and a distinctive quality of life. Its wine regions reflect a close relationship between viticulture, gastronomy, tourism and everyday life, offering a unique perspective on Patagonian wines.

Patagonia, Neuquén Province and a way of life

Patagonia is more than a place, it is an idea shaped by wind, distance and vast open landscapes. Within this immense territory, Neuquén Province stands out for its contrasts, where the Andes meet the desert and rivers bring life to arid plains.

Wine regions of Neuquén

San Patricio del Chañar

The most established wine region of the province, known for its structured, fresh and balanced Patagonian wines.

Confluencia

The historical origin of viticulture in Neuquén, shaped by small vineyards and agricultural traditions.

Ribera del Limay, Senillosa and Picún Leufú

An emerging river influenced zone, focused on freshness, tension and expressive wines.

Cutral Co and Plaza Huincul

A singular wine landscape in the heart of the Patagonian plateau, defined by resilience and experimentation.

Alto Neuquén, Norte Neuquino

The most remote wine territory, where viticulture remains deeply connected to memory and place.

An invitation to taste Neuquén

Neuquén wines are best discovered alongside regional cuisine, traditional Argentine beef cuts, Patagonian lamb, fresh river trout and local gastronomy, always framed by vast landscapes and genuine hospitality.

Sergio Landoni is a Sommelier de Territorio based in San Martín de los Andes, dedicated to communicating the wines of Neuquén and Patagonia.
